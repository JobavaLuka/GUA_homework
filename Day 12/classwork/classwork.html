Apps:

 TikTok

TikTok is a social media app centered on short-form videos, often set to music or trending sounds. It's known for viral challenges, creative edits, and algorithm-driven content discovery.
Users can create, share, and interact with videos, building audiences through likes, comments, duets, and stitches.

Instagram

Instagram is a visual-focused platform where users share photos, videos, reels, and stories. It blends personal updates with brand promotion, influencer marketing, and creative expression.
Features like filters, hashtags, and explore pages help content reach wider audiences, while direct messages allow private conversations.

YouTube

YouTube is the world's largest video-sharing platform, hosting everything from tutorials and reviews to music videos and livestreams.
Creators can monetize content through ads, memberships, and sponsorships. Viewers can subscribe, comment, like, and share, making it a hub for both entertainment and education.
