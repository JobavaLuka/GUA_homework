The Echidna

The echidna is one of the strangest mammals on Earth. At first glance it looks like a mix between a hedgehog and an anteater, but it's actually something much more unusual. Echidnas belong to a tiny group of animals called monotremes, which are mammals that lay eggs instead of giving birth to live babies.

There are only a few monotremes left in the world today, and most of them live in Australia and New Guinea. Echidnas are famous for their sharp spines, long sticky tongues, and their ability to dig incredibly fast. When threatened, they can bury themselves in the ground in seconds, leaving only their spines exposed.

What Makes Echidnas So Weird?

  • They lay eggs even though they are mammals.
  • They don't have teeth and use their tongue to catch ants and termites.
  • Their tongue can move in and out about 100 times per minute.
  • They have electroreceptors in their snout that help detect prey underground.
  • Echidnas and platypuses are the only mammals that lay eggs
  • Baby echidnas are called puggles.

Where They Live

Echidnas live mainly in Australia and New Guinea. They are extremely adaptable animals and can survive in forests, deserts, mountains, and even colder environments. During the day they usually hide under logs, rocks, or inside burrows to stay safe from predators.

Even though they look slow and clumsy, echidnas are surprisingly strong diggers and good swimmers. They spend most of their time searching for ants and termites, which make up the majority of their diet.

Fun Fact: Knuckles Is Also an Echidna

If you've played the Sonic games, you might recognize the name Knuckles. The red character from the Sonic series is actually designed as an echidna.

Of course, the real animal can't punch through walls or glide through the air like the character, but the idea came from the echidna's tough spines and strong digging claws.
